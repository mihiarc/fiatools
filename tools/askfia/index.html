See It In Action

You
How much timber volume is in North Carolina's forests?
askFIA
Based on the latest FIA survey data, North Carolina has approximately 45.2 billion cubic feet of net timber volume across all forest land. This includes 18.5 billion cubic feet of softwood species (primarily loblolly and longleaf pine) and 26.7 billion cubic feet of hardwood species.
You
What about just the oak species?
askFIA
Oak species in North Carolina account for 8.3 billion cubic feet of timber volume. The most common species are white oak (2.1B ft³), red oak (1.9B ft³), and chestnut oak (1.4B ft³).

Key Features

Natural Language Interface

Ask questions the way you naturally would. No need to learn query syntax, database schemas, or programming languages.

Conversational Context

Follow up on previous questions without restating everything. askFIA remembers the context of your conversation.

Plain English Answers

Get responses in clear, understandable language with relevant context and units of measurement.

Official FIA Data

All answers come from the authoritative USDA Forest Service Forest Inventory and Analysis database.

Multi-Metric Queries

Ask about volume, carbon, biomass, area, tree counts, and more in a single conversation.

Geographic Filtering

Focus on specific states, counties, or regions. Compare data across different geographic areas.
